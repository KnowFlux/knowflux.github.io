A Calling

Exploded rushed after Serpsight, flying through the winding passageways. Although he was familiar with the way, Serpsight chose a different route—perhaps a shorter path? The twists and turns seemed random, but before long, the passages opened up into a vast cavern. It stretched for thousands of tail-lengths in every direction, the ceiling hanging impossibly high above. Where were they? Exploded had never seen the cavern before, but it seemed to be for meetings with enormous audiences.

Then he noticed his surroundings, and Exploded's eyes widened in astonishment. How many dragons filled this cavern? Hundreds, maybe even thousands. The realization struck him: could every single dragon in the Fittus be here? The notion felt absurd, foreign to him. This had never - never - happened before.

An excited hum swelled from the gathered dragons, the tension palpable. The noise was insistent - a deafening, dull roar which echoed off the walls, sounds and noises repeating and bouncing, stabbing into Exploded's ears like so many spears, a relentless onslaught he almost couldn't bear. But he was saved from enduring the torment when the commander appeared.

Initially, Exploded didn’t notice; a sudden hush had fallen over the throng. It wasn’t until a sharp red light flickered across his vision that he looked up, locking eyes with the commander. A shiver ran down his spine, and he averted his gaze.

The commander took his position at the forefront of the cave, hovering above the sea of dragons, his imposing form washed in torchlight. The light danced across his scales, refracting in dazzling patterns, blurred slightly by the soft mist that drifted through the air.

The tension reached its climax as the commander surveyed the crowd, still and silent. Every eye focused intently on him, the air thick and heavy with suspense, a collective breath held in anticipation - then, at last, released as the commander began to speak.

"I have gathered you here for a matter of great importance. No doubt, there are many among you who wonder what the purpose of a meeting of such a grand scale might be."

The crowd seemed to murmur their agreement, tones of curiosity whispering over the crowd.

"It has come to my attention that a conspiracy bent on destroying the Fittus from the inside has infiltrated our stronghold."

This time, it was shock that rose from the gathered dragons. But Exploded was numb with realization: this was the test that the commander had mentioned.

"Every able dragon in the Fittus is charged with their uprooting!" The commander's voice was commanding, pressing down from his position, circling the highest reaches of the cave.

The words stilled the crowd to silence. Never had it occurred that anyone except for distinguished, noble-blooded warriors were given these tasks of espionage and battle.

But cutting through the silence, crashing through his thoughts, were the commander's words.

"From this day forward, rank will be earned through trials — real deeds. Hunts, missions, and trials that reveal who a dragon is, not who tradition claims they should be."

A second, harsh flicker of red light and Exploded shook himself back to the present, looking for the second time directly into the commander's eyes as he disappeared into the mist.

It was then that the noise began anew.
