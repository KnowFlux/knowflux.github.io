He scanned the street for pedestrians. None were in sight. He shut his eyes and with his remaining strength, forced his mind to empty. Focus. Focus. The pain in his head grew unbearably, but he ignored it, even as it clamored for attention

He reached deep within his mind, feeling the rhythm that he as a whole pulsed to and with. The rhythm that was him, which every atom in his body contributed to, each an instrument to a complete, grand symphony. But he forced the instruments apart, so that the grand symphony was less cohesive, buzzing more distantly.

He opened his eyes, which sparked with energy, breathed deeply, and stepped through the solid door and into the Itineror. He collapsed, drained, into the front seat, but he had more to do. Pulling up his feet, he kicked at the base of the control panel, angling the blow towards the dent he saw. The panel jerked and loosened, and it took twenty more seconds and several more kicks before it finally came loose. But he had wasted precious time: the clicking grew louder, its sound menacing and violent. He reached into the mass of circuitry below, manipulating the wiring, his hands shaking slightly with tension. He wasn’t fast enough, he had to be faster, but he couldn't afford any mistakes. A full minute was gone before he was close to finishing.

Around him, the sound reached its climax. The rhythm of the electronic sounds and pulses, of the pistons, grew an octave. With it, his head was agony. His vision blurred red, and fire roared behind his eyes. He clutched weakly at the wires - he was so close - but it was to no avail. His hands were thick and clumsy, and oh he couldn’t think…

Too late,

He thought brokenly.

Too late too late too late...

So much to do. Too much to do. Mend the fractures. Make illusions real. But his thoughts spiraled down, shattering into an empty void.

Fractures mend illusions unreal prime real core empty so empty pain. PAIN. Death dying IT… can’t… it…

The sounds seemed to distance themselves from him, even the ripping, screeching, tearing sound that vibrated and throbbed. Then came an unbearable, soul-ripping pain, that seemed to at once be him, a part of him, but so far too…

It tore himself from him, but with the separation came connection. He felt the core now, singing to reality. Singing to the memory of past realities. He chose a memory, allowing it to fill his fading self. Allowing its rhythm to be broadcast through a shadow of his existence. To it, he added an essence. The essence of what it was to be real. And as a new, shattered reality was born…

The Realist was no more.
