A Calling

A soft, warm light flickered around him, and he was lying on smooth stone. He glanced around himself. He was in a cave – his cave, he noticed. He felt relaxed and rested, and wondered what the time was. He certainly was up to spending the morning hours flying, if time allowed. He shivered slightly as a strange feeling overcame him – had he not done this already?

No, he had not – he’d just woken up this morning. The day before, he’d had that strange meeting with the commander. He shivered again, remembering the conversation. But he banished such thoughts from his head and lifted himself off the floor.

But the feeling persisted, only growing stronger as he stood. He was almost certain he had already spent the morning flying. So then why couldn’t he remember it? It was important, whatever had happened while he was flying. But no, that wasn’t possible – he’d just woken up.

Something else occurred to him. Red was a significant color while he was flying. But why would red be significant? He remembered something now. He’d flown into the jet streams. He’d followed two dragons, they’d entered a cave, and then there was red.

He shook himself. Why would he do something so foolish as to fly into a jet stream? He hadn’t even gone outside yet. But the two dragons… they existed, didn’t they? But he’d never seen them before, but in his mind he could recall how each looked.

A slight confusion drifted over him. What had he been thinking about? Yes, two dragons, and jet streams. His confusion increased – why would he have been thinking about jet streams? And which two dragons could he possibly have referred to? Right now he wanted to spend the morning flying. So where had jet streams and dragons come in? He couldn’t remember.

Feeling slightly dizzy, Exploded stumbled. Slowly, he lowered himself to the floor, and closed his eyes, and breathed deeply to clear his mind. The dizziness only increased, and a fog poured through his mind, clouding his thoughts from him, and darkness barred him from the world.

Exploded opened his eyes. A soft light danced on the cave walls around him, and the ground underneath was flat, cold stone. He felt rested and relaxed, and wondered what the time was. He intended to spend the morning flying, if time allowed. He truly hoped that it was still morning – he would have some time to spend in enjoyment, after the strange meeting with the commander the day before.

He shivered, remembering the conversation, but banished such thoughts from his mind, brightening at the prospect of going outside. Exploded stood slowly, and walked towards the exit, but was stopped abruptly when a messenger appeared into view.

Exploded knew the messenger – his name was Serpsight. “Meeting in the main hall. It’s about to begin, and you have barely any time!” Serpsight’s voice was urgent, and slightly annoyed, and the messenger turned and sprinted into the maze of hallways.

Disappointment flooded into Exploded, and he wished that he’d woken earlier. Meetings only happened in the afternoon. However, Exploded wasted no time in following Serpsight at a sharp pace. He did not want to be late for a meeting, especially one that sounded so urgent.
