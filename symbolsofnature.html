Symbols of Nature

A Haiku

Sky arcs overhead

Blanketing the Earth

Always protecting

A quiet pond, rippling

Sunlight plays across the waves

A symbol of peace

Stones slow the waves

Which journey ever for shore

A burst of color

Haikus

Haikus (HI â€“ koo) are an ancient form of Japanese poetry. A haiku consists of three lines: the first is five syllables long, the second is seven syllables long, and the third line is again five syllables long. Haikus usually talk about a single thing in a single moment of time. The poem above is a series of three haikus detailing a scene in nature.
