The cavern walls didn't echo; they vibrated, almost roared, the reverberations sinking into his skin and rattling the delicate bones behind his ears. The shouts of a thousand throats slammed into his chest like a physical blow, a wall of sound that split into jagged spears - sharp clicks of teeth and the long rasps of hisses.

But the noise didn't fade; it curdled. The full weight of the commander's accusations settled over the crowd like waves of ice. Heads swung, eyes filled with malice tracked the movements of lifelong den-mates. In the space of a split second, a clan became a wave of snouts waiting to strike.

The walls pressed down around him. His muscles were tense, waves of heat radiating off the dragons sucking the oxygen away. The acrid scent of fear filled his nostrils, the sulfurous scent twisting inside his lungs.  

The brilliant blue lines snapped into his vision, slicing through the mass of scales and talons. As the light swept across his vision, his muscles relaxed. His shoulders settled, and the frantic twitching of his tail-tip calmed.
